Howdy people!

Thanks so much for connecting in here at andymcgrath.com.

In my bio on the website, I mentioned being in The MacTones a few years back and thought I must find that music video and have it on here. This was the song that really got me kickstarted recording my songs that had been banking up for a while.

Tony Kemp and I were chatting from our houses during Covid and we were both passionate about having a high quality to our recordings — and as soon as we were able to mingle again we headed into Matrix Digital Studio to record his drums. Duncan McCann, me ol’ mate, came down from Palmy North and did a scintillating bass line to this track. We recorded 3 tracks and I went off to put my guitars, harmonica and additional vocal tracks at home.

I came back to do a final mix with a large number of individual tracks at Matrix and drove them absolutely bonkers with my micromanagement of each track. They even sat me down at the end and said, “I can’t do music like that!” Well I did... and I do. It made me realise I have to get better at doing this myself... and that’s another chapter :).

Please take a watch and listen to this track. One of my absolute favs — singing about being in an altered state in my twenties and walking around a park at 2am in the morning with a mate, bringing back all this wet grass on our shoes and trousers which we didn’t see on the carpet until he went to leave when the sun came up!

Fun times. Enjoy. 🎸
